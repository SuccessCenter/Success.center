Course Description

A specialized educational program that qualifies technicians to inspect, repair, and maintain the mechanical, electrical, and electronic systems in electric and hybrid vehicles. The curriculum covers electric drive systems, high-voltage batteries, control units, and system programming, with a strong emphasis on practical workshops.

Study Options & Schedule

  • Study Options: On-Campus / Online
  • Class Times: Morning / Evening

Topics and Benefits

Electric Drive Systems

Study electric motors and control units.

High-Voltage Battery Systems

Diagnose and maintain lithium batteries and management systems.

Hybrid Control Systems

Understand hybrid operation mechanisms and control units.

Diagnostic Equipment

Learn to use advanced diagnostic tools.

Programming

Learn to program and optimize vehicle systems.

Safety

Focus on high-voltage and battery safety procedures.
